You don’t need a college degree

Don’t take my word for it. Even tech heavyweights like Google, Apple, and IBM no longer require employees to have a degree. Many smaller software companies have been this way for years: they care more about the skills you have than where you learned them. It’s an increasingly large talent pool: 24% of professional developers who took Stack Overflow’s 2018 survey said that they don’t have a college degree.

Colleges have long struggled to teach up-to-date practices in web design and development. Their curriculum approval process simply can’t keep up with the rate of change. If you’re researching a program to attend, the best focus on technology-independent principles, but use current and upcoming approaches to teach it.
